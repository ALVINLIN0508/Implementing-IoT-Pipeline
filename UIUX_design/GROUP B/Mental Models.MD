# Mapping and aligning mental models

## Core interaction selected
I selected the core interaction:
A company searches for suitable students on the RDI platform.
This task represents the main value of the system and strongly influences whether collaboration can begin.

## User expectations
Company expectations
How they expect the task to work:
Companies expect that entering a keyword such as “AI”, “UI design”, or “Python” will immediately show a list of relevant students. They assume the system will highlight the most suitable candidates automatically and that profiles will be easy to compare.
What actions they believe are possible:
Searching by keywords
Filtering students by skills or project type
Viewing structured student profiles
Contacting students directly

Student expectations
How they expect the task to work:
Students expect that once they complete their profile, companies will be able to find them easily. They believe adding skills and keywords increases their visibility and that companies will understand their abilities based on the information provided.
What actions they believe are possible:
Editing their profile
Adding skills and keywords
Uploading past project experience
Browsing company projects

Teacher expectations
How they expect the task to work:
Teachers expect the platform to reduce manual matching work. They assume student profiles will follow a consistent structure.
What actions they believe are possible:
Approving company accounts
Publishing announcements
Ensuring student profiles meet required standards

## Documented expectations
Companies expect fast and accurate matching.
Students expect to be discoverable after completing their profiles.
Teachers expect standardized information and reduced manual work.

## Comparison with the actual system structure
The current system design includes:
Keyword‑based search
Three user roles (student, company, teacher)
Structured profile fields
Teacher‑controlled announcements
A simple UI
No automated recommendation or ranking system

## Identified mismatches
Mismatch 1: Students expect to be found automatically
Students believe completing their profile is enough, but the system requires companies to actively search.
Mismatch 2: Companies expect clear, comparable profiles
Companies expect structured information, but students may enter inconsistent or incomplete data.

## Proposed design changes
Interface cues
Add a short explanation under the search bar:
“Results are based on keyword matching.”
Add hints in the student profile editor:
“Companies search using your keywords.”
Terminology adjustments
Rename “Search” to “Find suitable students”
Workflow adjustments
Introduce a structured profile template with required fields
Add a “profile completeness” indicator
Add simple ranking or sorting options 
Add filters for skills, availability, and project type
Add automatic reminders for missing profile information
These changes help align the system with user expectations and reduce confusion during the matching process.
