# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

## Project Context
This project aims to design an RDI platform that connects companies with university students for project-based collaboration. Current collaboration processes are slow, unclear, and require significant manual effort from both sides. The platform seeks to reduce this friction by helping companies quickly identify suitable students and enabling students to present their skills in a structured and understandable way.

## Method Choice
The JTBD framework is appropriate for this early-stage project because it focuses on users’ underlying goals rather than predefined features. This aligns with the course emphasis on understanding real problems before proposing solutions. JTBD helps avoid feature-driven thinking and ensures that design decisions support meaningful outcomes for both companies and students.

## Identified User Jobs
Company perspective
When a company needs support for a development task or RDI project,
I want to quickly identify and evaluate suitable students based on reliable information,
So I can start collaboration with confidence and without wasting time on unclear communication.
Emotional drivers:
Reduce uncertainty about student skills
Feel confident that the selected student can deliver
Avoid administrative frustration

Student perspective 
When I am searching for practical experience or a thesis-related project,
I want to present my skills, interests, and availability in a clear and structured way,
So I can be matched with relevant opportunities that support my studies and future career.
Emotional drivers:
Feel confident that companies understand my abilities
Reduce anxiety about “not knowing what companies want”
Gain a sense of progress toward graduation and employment

## Obstacles and Pain Points
Company pain points
Student information is often unstructured or incomplete
Difficult to compare students or evaluate suitability
Communication with students can be slow or unclear
Existing platforms focus on recruitment, not project-based collaboration
Student pain points
Uncertainty about how to present skills in a way companies understand
Lack of visibility into available projects
Fear of being overlooked due to unclear profiles
Difficulty matching personal interests with company needs

## How This Informs My Design
Applying JTBD helps shift the design focus from “adding features” to “supporting outcomes.”
This directly influenced several design decisions in my project:
Clear user roles (student, company, teacher) reduce confusion and support trust
Keyword-based search helps both sides quickly find relevant matches
Structured profiles help students communicate skills in a way companies can evaluate
Simple UI reduces cognitive load and supports confident decision-making
Teacher/admin-controlled announcements ensure reliable information flow
These choices directly address the functional and emotional jobs identified above and help reduce the friction that currently prevents effective collaboration.
